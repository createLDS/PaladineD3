Having breakfast in Korea

Nowadays, Koreans have started adopting Western breakfasts such as bakery items, cereal, etc. Many families still eat a traditional breakfast which usually consists of rice, several banchan, a soup of some kind, and a meat dish.

Chuseok

Chuseok (once known as hangawi) is a major harvest festival that lasts 3 days. It is celebrated on the 15th day of the 8th month of the lunar calendar on the full moon.

As a celebration of the good harvest, Koreans visit their ancestral hometowns and share a feast of Korean traditional food such as songpyeon and rice wines.
